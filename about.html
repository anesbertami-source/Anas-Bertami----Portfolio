About Me

A curious mind from Morocco, obsessed with how things break — so I can learn to build them stronger.

Who I am

Student. Builder.
Security Thinker.

Hey — I'm Anasse Bertami, a 21-year-old computer science student currently in my second year at university Mundiapolis. What started as pure curiosity about how computers work quickly turned into a passion for understanding how they can be broken, secured, and protected.

My journey into cybersecurity began when I stumbled across my first CTF challenge. The mix of puzzle-solving, technical depth, and real-world impact hooked me completely. Since then, I've been learning everything from network protocols and cryptography to web application security and ethical hacking methodologies.

Outside of coursework, I spend time on platforms like TryHackMe and HackTheBox, work on personal security tools in Python, and build web projects that let me see applications from both a developer's and an attacker's perspective.

My next goal: earn my Bachelor's degree in Cybersecurity and land a role where I can protect real systems, real data, and real people.
